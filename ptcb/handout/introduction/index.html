Life as a developer

Even a senior developer is likely to only master the art of a few languages, there are simply too many out there to understand them all. However, as a good developer you will be able to understand new technologies, languages or frameworks in a short time frame and will be able to teach them yourself. Our aim as a coding bootcamp is not to train you to become a perfect developer, but to teach you how to acquire the necessary knowledge as quickly as possible. It might very well be the case that later you won’t work with Node.js, React or PostgreSQL (skills we are teaching here in our full-time software engeering course) but Python or Angluar instead. However, having attended our bootcamp you should be able to quickly learn those different languages/frameworks.

Google

There is such a wealth of information out there especially related to coding, that you will find an answer to almost any question. You will most certainly find a lot of resources on StackOverflow, where you’ll probably end up through Google. We also encourage you to read blog articles about coding since many good developers have a blog where they write about their daily problem solving tasks and the latest technology you need to catch up on. Having a blog helps developers get their name out and build up credibility, so these can be a good source of information.

When you Google it is always better to be as specific as you can. javascript error callback function doesn’t really help you as much as javascript setTimeout error "callback" argument must be a function. It makes more sense to type out whole sentences or questions into Google, something that some people always seem a bit surprised of.
